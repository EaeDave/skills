# Timeline report

How something evolved over time. The reader's question: *what changed, when,
and why?*

## Sections, in order

1. **Resumo** — one paragraph: what is being traced and the arc of its
   evolution (where it started, where it stands).
2. **Linha do tempo** — one Mermaid `timeline` diagram: periods or dates as
   sections, business-visible changes as events. Fall back to a vertical list
   of date-stamped cards when events need more than a phrase each.
3. **Marcos** — one card per significant change: when, what changed in
   business terms, and why (the driver: nova regra, correção, demanda legal).
4. **Estado atual** — bullets: the behavior as it stands today, so the reader
   never has to mentally replay the timeline to know the present.
5. **Pendências** — bullets for announced-but-unfinished changes. Omit if
   none.

## Guidance

- Anchor every event to evidence found during legwork (a decision, release,
  or dated change) — an event with no date and no driver is a guess, not a
  milestone.
- Describe each change by its effect on behavior, not by the act of changing
  ("passou a exigir aprovação dupla acima de R$ 10.000", not "regra
  atualizada").
